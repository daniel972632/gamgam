# Gamgam Design System

## Brand Essence

Gamgam captures the feeling of anticipation before a trip.

The product should feel:

* Exciting
* Optimistic
* Collaborative
* Lightweight
* Playful
* Modern

Never feel:

* Corporate
* Enterprise
* Spreadsheet-like
* Overly technical

---

# Emotional Goal

Users should feel:

"I can't wait for this trip."

The product experience should amplify anticipation rather than logistics.

---

# Design Principles

## Visual First

Images are more important than text.

Destinations should feel inspiring.

---

## Simple Collaboration

Collaboration should feel effortless.

Users should not think about permissions or workflow complexity.

---

## Fast Planning

Actions should require minimal clicks.

Adding destinations should feel instant.

---

## Friendly Interface

Rounded corners.

Soft shadows.

Comfortable spacing.

No harsh visual elements.

---

# Design References

Primary References:

* Airbnb
* Notion
* Pinterest

Secondary References:

* Arc Browser
* Linear
* Apple Maps

Avoid:

* Jira
* Trello
* Enterprise dashboards

---

* CTA
* Active states
* Highlights

---

## Secondary

* Background surfaces

---

## Neutral

Dark Gray

Purpose:

* Text

## Semantic

* Green

Warning:

* Amber

* Red

* Blue

---

# Typography

Style:

* Clean
* Modern
* Highly readable

Preferred Fonts:

English:

* Inter

Korean:

* Pretendard

---

# Border Radius

Cards should feel approachable.

Recommended:

* Large radius
* Rounded containers

---

# Shadows

Soft and subtle.

Avoid heavy elevation.

---

# Components

## Trip Card

Contains:

* Cover image
* Destination
* Date range
* Member count

---

## Schedule Card

Contains:

* Place image
* Place name
* Time
* Category
* Memo

---

## Member Avatar

Small circular avatars.

Show collaborative presence.

---

## Map Marker

Simple.

Minimal.

Category-aware.

---

# Layout

Desktop:

* Left Sidebar
* Main Content
* Optional Right Panel

Mobile:

* Bottom Navigation
* Full-width Cards

---

# Motion

Motion should communicate progress.

Examples:

* Card reordering
* Schedule updates
* Member activity

Animations:

* Fast
* Smooth
* Purposeful

Avoid excessive motion.

---

# Empty States

Empty screens should encourage planning.

Examples:

"No plans yet."

"Start building your trip."

"Where should we go first?"

---

# Illustration Style

Minimal.

Modern.

Travel-oriented.

Avoid cartoon-heavy graphics.

---

# Product Personality

If Gamgam were a person:

* Loves planning trips
* Shares recommendations
* Organized but not strict
* Excited about future experiences

The product should feel like a travel-savvy friend helping organize ideas.
